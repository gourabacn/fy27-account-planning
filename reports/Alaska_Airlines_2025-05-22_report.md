# FY27 Account Planning Pre-Read — Alaska Airlines
## Prepared for: Client Account Lead | Respondents: 1 | Session: 2025-05-22

---

## 01 · How the client sees us
Alaska acknowledges our contribution at Hawaiian Airlines but views us as unproven at Alaska specifically. CIO Vikram Baskaran sees Accenture as "an impactful advisor who comes at a high cost"—he recognizes our value and quality but questions the investment. The client perceives us as "bold and too confident" which clashes with Alaska's culture of priding themselves on their achievements and reluctance to acknowledge shortcomings. We are seen as expensive and risky compared to their current "safe and cheap" providers.

## 02 · Our biggest risk of losing relevance
**Inertia is the #1 risk.** Alaska operates with an "order taker mindset" and traditionally buys from IPPs. They favor smaller boutique firms that land and expand over large deal constructs requiring transformation. With the merger, we risk losing existing book of business. Cognizant sits at the "sweet spot" with the CIO, making them the incumbent advantage we must overcome.

## 03 · What we should stop doing
**Stop insisting on large strategic deals as our entry strategy.** If we're unwilling to work on smaller strategic deals to build trust within the IT organization, we should abandon this account entirely. Our sales approach of demanding $5M+ commitments upfront is fundamentally misaligned with how Alaska wants to buy. We must be prepared to land and expand rather than forcing large transformational deals.

## 04 · The client relationship
**We have one primary contact:** Vikram Baskaran (CIO), the primary decision maker for backend systems and most IT initiatives. **Critical gap:** We're missing Charu Jain (CMO), Vikram's peer who controls all customer-facing IT. This represents a major blind spot—we only have half the technology organization covered. Our influence is limited to one executive who already has a preferred provider.

## 05 · Where we are underinvested
**Biggest gaps:** Cloud and infrastructure capabilities. **Most important contribution:** Being an impactful advisor, but Vikram questions whether the high cost justifies the value during a critical merger transition period. He's concerned about "putting all eggs in one basket" with a firm he hasn't worked with extensively, especially when he has Cognizant as a known, reliable alternative.

## 06 · White space
**Two clear opportunities:** IT organization under Vikram and customer-facing IT under Charu. **Ecosystem play:** Microsoft partnership has momentum. The white space exists across both backend and frontend systems, but we need relationships with both leaders to capitalize.

## 07 · The mega deal
**Coherent $50M+ opportunity exists:** Application Management Services, Infrastructure Management Services, AIOps, and CX enablement (AI Governance, Change management post-PSS, API usage). The opportunity is there, but no consensus on approach—it requires building from smaller engagements rather than selling as a mega deal upfront.

## 08 · Threat board
**Cognizant is the primary threat.** They currently handle IMS, AMS, and all app development work for Vikram. They've achieved the preferred position by being "safe and cheap" with a land-and-expand model. For Alaska to choose them over us, they simply need to maintain status quo—they're already the incumbent with proven delivery and lower cost.

## 09 · Our internal obstacle
**Sales team compensation and focus structure is broken.** We discourage sales professionals from working deals under $5M, which prevents relationship building necessary for larger deals later. Our inflexibility in adapting to client buying preferences and inability to support relationship-building for smaller engagements will kill our chances. The sales team wants to work "only large projects > $5M"—this approach guarantees failure at Alaska.

## 10 · Bold moves and session priorities
**Bold move:** Approach Vikram with integrated Cloud Migration, AIOps with IMS and AMS, plus AI-led SDLC to drive savings, efficiency, speed to market, and resilience. **Critical question:** Are we ready to pivot—either meet the client where they are or influence them to think more strategically? **Facilitator focus:** How to land and expand effectively.

## 11 · What this session must do
This session must answer whether Accenture will fundamentally change our sales approach for Alaska Airlines or walk away from the account. Success means committing to a land-and-expand strategy with smaller initial engagements to build trust, even if it conflicts with our standard sales model. Failure means continuing to push large deals that Alaska won't buy, ensuring Cognizant retains and expands their position while we remain irrelevant.
